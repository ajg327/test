Competitive Super Smash Brothers Ultimate

Super Smash Brothers Ultimate is the newest game in the Smash Brothers series and is a very popular competitive esport. The scene is fairly new and is growing larger and larger.

Top Characters

Joker: Joker was the first DLC character of Ultimate and is in contention for the best character in the game due to his good combo game and buff gimmick. Joker has a meter next to his character portrait that when full allows him to gain buffs to his attack power and special moves. His base specials are Grappling Hook (up special), Eiha (side special), Rebel's Guard (down special), and Gun (nuetral special). His buffed specials are Wings of Rebellion (Up Special), Eihagon (side special), Tetrakarn/Makarakarn (down special), and Gun Special (neutral special).
Peach: Peach is a difficult character to learn, but very rewarding. Very strong in competitive play, Peach is defined by her high percentage combos and fast gameplay. Her specials are Peach Parasol (up special), Peach Bomber (side special), Vegetable (down special), and Toad (neutral special).
Pikachu: Also a very hard character to master, Pikachu could be considered the best character in the game. Pikachu's base hits don't do much damage, but its real talent are the combos it can perform. Its specials are Quick Attack (up special), Skull Bash (side special), Thunder (down special), and Thunder Jolt (neutral special).

Competitive Stages

Pokemon Stadium 2: Without stage hazzards, this stage is very intersting. The platforms on each side of the stage can be used for combo potential or even early KO set ups for some characters. It's a very popular stage because of this!
Town and City: A relatively basic stage, the main gimmick of this stage is a small platform which can appear from time to time allowing for combo potential for certain characters. Keep in mind the platform isn't reliable because if you're on it when it leaves, it'll head towards the blast zone. Make sure you get off it quickly before it reaches the blast zone!
Yoshi's Story: This stage returns from Melee with a few differences. Because Smash Ultimate allows for the players to turn off stage hazzards, the stage no longer has the tiny platform revolving around it. Instead, players make use of the walls of the stage to create opportunities for revovery.
